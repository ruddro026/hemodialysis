# hemodialysis
The management of chronic kidney disease 
(CKD) is especially difficult in areas with little access to 
traditional hemodialysis machines and little funding for 
healthcare. This work aims to develop an automated system for 
heparin administration, controlling the properties of the 
dialysate and monitoring patient vital parameters during 
hemodialysis. The system ensures accurate composition and the 
right fluid mixing by automating the dialysate fluid transport to 
the dialyzer. Dialysate temperature and flow rate are 
continuously monitored to optimize treatment conditions, and 
patient safety throughout the dialysis session is improved by 
real-time evaluation of arterial and venous pressures in 
conjunction with heart rate. To prevent negative consequences, 
the temperature of the dialysate fluid must match the blood 
temperature, which is critical for safety. Treatment effectiveness 
depends on maintaining a suitable flow rate in sync with the 
dialyzer's blood flow. Integrating a smart syringe pump adds 
sophistication to this work by automatically administering 
heparin to prevent blood clot formation and measuring heparin 
levels to ensure optimal dosing. By implementing this system, 
hemodialysis could be made more accessible to CKD patients so 
they can undergo treatment conveniently at home or in remote 
areas. It could reduce equipment expenses and improve patient 
outcomes by monitoring their vital signs.
